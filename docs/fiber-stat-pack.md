# Fiber guide stat pack (review before publish)

This is the data that will populate the hero stat + environmental tiles + ethics section on each `/guide/[slug]` page. **Review the flagged numbers, then I roll it onto the pages.** Nothing here is public yet.

## How to read this
- **Hero stat** = the one hand-picked animated number that leads the page.
- **4 tiles** (same on every fiber): Water (L/kg) · Carbon (kg CO₂e/kg) · Biodegradable · Sheds plastic microfibres.
- **Villain** = the one worse fiber each is compared against.
- **Ethics** = only added where there's a real, sourced story.
- 🟢 solid · 🟡 contested (show as a range or with a caveat) · 🔴 thin/disputed (needs your call).

**The honest meta-point:** textile LCA numbers are a genuine swamp. There is no agreed system boundary, so the same fiber shows a 4-30x spread across studies. That's exactly why we (a) show ranges not false precision, (b) cite every number, and (c) flag the shaky ones here instead of laundering them into clean-looking figures. This protects the trust brand.

## Per-fiber detail

### Linen 🟢 clean story, 🔴 carbon number
- **Hero:** *34,000 years* — flax fibres found in a Georgian cave are the oldest fibre humans are known to have used, dyed and spun during the Ice Age. (Harvard Gazette)
- **Water:** ~300–1,000 L/kg, mostly rain-fed European flax (no irrigation). 🟡 sources also show higher when finishing water is counted.
- **Carbon:** ~0.5–2.5 kg. 🔴 **biggest spread in the pack** (studies range to 11+ depending on fertiliser). Show as a range and name the driver (fertiliser), or lead the tile with water instead.
- **Biodeg:** Yes (weeks to months, undyed). **Microfibre:** No.
- **Ethics:** none. Optional quiet positive: European Flax certification requires fair pay + humane conditions.

### Organic cotton 🟡 headline numbers disputed
- **Hero:** cotton is *2.4% of farmland but 6–16% of pesticides* — organic uses none. (EJF)
- **Water:** ~91% less blue (irrigation) water than conventional. 🟡 **caveat:** largely because organic is grown in rain-fed regions, not a property of "organic" itself; total water is closer to conventional.
- **Carbon:** ~46% lower than conventional (≈3–3.5 kg). 🔴 **flag:** this 91%/46% pair comes from one 2014 Textile Exchange LCA that Norway's Consumer Authority ruled misleading when applied to specific garments. Show with the caveat or soften to "grown without synthetic pesticides."
- **Biodeg:** Yes. **Microfibre:** No. **Villain:** conventional cotton.
- **Ethics — YOUR CALL:** no forced-labor story, BUT in 2022 GOTS uncovered ~20,000 tonnes of *fake* organic-cotton certificates in India (cloned sites, fake QR codes). For a trust brand this is a compelling "why verification matters" angle. Include as an integrity note, or omit?

### Cotton (conventional) 🟢 strong ethics
- **Hero:** *~2,700 litres* of water for one t-shirt, about what one person drinks in 2.5 years. (WWF) — concrete, famous.
- **Water:** ~3,000–10,000+ L/kg 🟡 (US ~2,250 efficient irrigation; India ~8,600). **Villain baseline = virgin polyester** (cotton can't be its own villain).
- **Carbon:** ~2–6 kg (Cotton Inc LCA ≈5.9). **Biodeg:** Yes. **Microfibre:** No.
- **Ethics — YES, strong:** (1) Xinjiang forced labor — ~20% of world cotton; basis of the US import ban. (2) Farmworker pesticide exposure. (Optional: Aral Sea collapse from cotton irrigation.) Well-sourced (New Lines, USITC, EJF).

### TENCEL lyocell 🟢 clean, 🟡 absolute numbers Lenzing-sourced
- **Hero:** *>99%* of the solvent and water is recovered and reused in the closed loop (vs open-loop viscose that dumps it). (Lenzing)
- **Water:** ~300–800 L/kg 🟡 (industry/Higg; no fully independent figure). **Carbon:** ~50% less than generic lyocell 🟡 (relative, not absolute).
- **Biodeg:** Yes (TÜV Austria certified, incl. marine). **Microfibre:** No (cellulosic, biodegrades). **Villain:** conventional viscose (sharper than polyester — isolates the process).
- **Ethics:** none. Positive: FSC/PEFC wood, top Canopy "green shirt" rank.

### Viscose / rayon 🔴 numbers contested, ethics strong
- **Hero:** *carbon disulfide* (CS₂), the core solvent, is linked across 150+ years of research to nerve damage, psychiatric illness, Parkinson's and heart disease in factory workers. (NCBI/PMC) — one of the longest-documented occupational harms in textiles.
- **Water:** ~300 to several thousand L/kg 🔴 (huge mill-to-mill variance). **Carbon:** ~2–11 kg 🔴 (region/method). Show as ranges + note "viscose is not one standard."
- **Biodeg:** Yes (caveat: not in sealed landfill; finishes complicate). **Microfibre:** No (cellulosic). **Villain:** virgin polyester (end-of-life persistence).
- **Ethics — YES:** CS₂ worker poisoning + Changing Markets "Dirty Fashion" wastewater/river contamination + ~⅓ of viscose historically from endangered forests (Canopy; improving).

### Bamboo (viscose) 🟢 great hook
- **Hero:** the FTC fined Amazon, Sears, Macy's and others *$1.5M+* for labelling rayon as "bamboo" — because bamboo's eco properties don't survive the chemical process. What's on the hanger is viscose. 🟡 verify FTC primary before quoting the exact $ figure.
- **Water / Carbon:** same as viscose (a 2025 LCA shows *processing*, not feedstock, drives impact). Key on-page point: bamboo viscose is **not** better than wood viscose.
- **Biodeg:** Yes. **Microfibre:** No. **Villain:** virgin polyester.
- **Ethics — YES:** same CS₂ worker harm + a distinct consumer-deception/greenwashing story (the FTC cases).

### Wool 🟢 biodeg hook, 🔴 carbon contested
- **Hero:** merino wool biodegrades *~95% in 15 weeks* in soil, feeding it nitrogen and sulfur. "It ends up back in the ground, not a landfill." (Journal of the Textile Institute / IWTO)
- **Water:** no clean single number 🔴 — use the green-water nuance (95%+ is rainfall on grazing land, not scarce freshwater).
- **Carbon:** ~10–30 kg 🔴 **must carry a methane caveat** (GWP100 vs GWP\* is actively disputed; ~75% of the figure is sheep methane). Do not publish a bare number.
- **Biodeg:** Yes. **Microfibre:** No. **Villain:** acrylic (fossil, sheds plastic, doesn't biodegrade).
- **Ethics — YES:** mulesing (skin removed from lambs, mostly Australia, NZ banned it 2018). RWS certification prohibits it (only ~4.3% of global wool). 🟡 pull the live mulesed % from AWEX before quoting.

### Silk 🔴 thinnest data in the pack
- **Hero — YOUR CALL:** either the visceral *~2,500 silkworms boiled per pound of silk* (on-brand shock, animal-welfare) OR *8,500 years* of human use (history). ⚠️ the "30,000 years" claim is NOT supported; best dating is 8,500.
- **Water:** ~600–27,000 L/kg 🔴 (the famous "worst fibre" figure traces to ONE small Indian study; couldn't independently verify). **Carbon:** ~2.4–81 kg 🔴 (single-study, 30x swing). Both need "high and highly variable" framing, not a number.
- **Biodeg:** Yes (protein fibre). **Microfibre:** No. **Villain:** polyester satin.
- **Ethics — YES but date it:** sericulture boils the pupae; peace/ahimsa silk avoids killing-for-harvest. Child labor: HRW documented 300,000+ bonded children — but that report is **2003, 23 years old**; flag as historical, current prevalence not well documented.

### Polyester 🟢 the solid one
- **Hero:** *59% of all fibre made in 2024 was polyester* (78 of 132M tonnes), 88% virgin fossil-based. "Nearly 6 in 10 garments made today are plastic." (Textile Exchange 2025) — **the most solid number in the pack, safe to lead with.**
- **Water:** low but 🔴 the circulated figures are disputed (Higg's "litres" is actually a mislabeled scarcity score). Recommend qualitative: "low direct water, but common figures are contested."
- **Carbon:** ~3.1 kg (current EF3.1 data — use this, not the old ~1.5–2). **Biodeg:** No (centuries; fragments to microplastic).
- **Microfibre:** **Yes** — a single 6kg wash sheds ~496,030 plastic microfibres. (Napper & Thompson, Univ. Plymouth) — great, hard citation.
- **Villain:** organic cotton (~2–3x lower carbon; directional). **Ethics — YES:** fossil-fuel upstream + garment-worker wages (present as two adjacent facts, not one causal chain).
